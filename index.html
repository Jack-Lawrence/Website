About Me

Hi, I'm Jack, a fourth-year Games Development student working towards a first-class degree. I'm an aspiring Systems Designer focused on creating unique, engaging experiences, holding an NC in Video Game Development and an HND in Digital Design & Development. I have built a strong technical foundation in Unity, C#, and GML.

I love refining complex mechanics through iteration to make gameplay feel both intuitive and meaningful. My dissertation explores how multimodal feedback—visual, auditory, and motion effects—influences player perception of weapon realism and satisfaction. Alongside this research, I am designing and programming as part of a team for a game we are entering into the upcoming Tranzfuzer competition. Outside of development, I'm usually at the gym, walking Munros, or playing the latest game releases!

Current Project

Kyomen

In Development

After placing 2nd at the largest Global Game Jam site in Scotland, I have moved into a multidisciplinary role to help refine our project for the upcoming Tranzfuzer competition. Due to our small team size, I have been bridging the gap between technical implementation and visual design, taking ownership of the HUD and UI art while simultaneously programming core systems. My work involves developing the Talent Tree, item and menu frameworks, and the persistence logic required to maintain data between environments. I also built the data logging systems to store player variables and settings, ensuring that the interface and the backend function as a single, cohesive unit.
